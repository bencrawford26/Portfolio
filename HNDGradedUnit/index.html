Welcome!

Hello, and welcome to my website! Created for the graded unit of my higher national diploma course in college - I designed this website to show my understanding of using the p5.js library and the genetic algorithm. The application you can see running to the right (or below, depending on your screen resolution) is an example of what the p5.js library is used for. This example converts polar coordinates (radius and theta) to cartesian coordinates (x and y) then draws an ellipse at each point and the radius and theta are incremented by an amount dependent on the sliders, finally for each frame of animation these coordinates are converted as p5.js can only recieve cartesian coordinates - each ellipse has its colour generated according to Perlin noise (a smooth random). I chose perlin noise over the basic random function so that the colours generated are always similar. Feel free to move the sliders around and see what kind of patterns can be generated!
